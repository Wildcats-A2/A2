Group Reflection

Our team comprised a total of 6 team members and all of us have thoroughly enjoyed working in this team that we are assigned to. Every member appears to be friendly and approachable. We also became more comfortable with each other after a few sessions of the meeting, which is one of the vital elements that we need when working as a group. We decided to separate the project into smaller tasks where everyone has their job delegated. As this is a group project, we all worked hand in hand to complete it. Everyone was willing in taking tasks that were allocated to us and were cooperative in completing them within the datelines given. Eventually we were able to complete all tasks assigned to us.
At the initial phase of the group meeting, two of our group members had issues with their Microsoft Teams. This had impacted their participation in the first few meetings. Therefore, we were not able to delegate the tasks much earlier during the mid semester break. We should have considered sending an email to all members of the meeting that will take place and ask if they could make sure their software is working. We believe through this experience, we would be more aware of the problems and circumstances that each team member might be facing.
The one thing that surprised the majority of us the most was how fast and efficiently we got our tasks done within the deadline that was set for everyone. All members were also active participants and prompt in their responses. Despite everyone’s busy schedules, we still managed to hold several meetings to keep track of everyone's progress and make sure it is on the right path.
We believe that a group assessment/project requires all members to learn to work cooperatively and effectively with each other. Not forgetting to mention, our group leader, Daniel has set a very good example by leading the group well and setting us focus on the tasks at hand. We would say he is very responsible and played his role well. He managed to keep the group well organised and conducted.
Well.... after the technical issues with Microsoft Teams were solved, all team members attended all the meetings held by our group leader. We also agreed that the Github log of activity could not reflect completely on what our group members have contributed to this project. Lastly, we would all admit that we couldn’t ask for a better team and team leader :) !!!

Daniel

I have worked with many different people and groups during my working career. At times there are barriers such as technology deficiencies, differing expectations, languages, levels of subject understanding or even competing egos, however this was surprisingly not the case here within the Wildcats.
I wasn’t sure how we would all get along, especially since our only meetings have been from behind a screen and there is no opportunity for building a rapport in person and not to mention the age gap (I’m at least a decade older than everyone in the group!). These initial thoughts disappeared within minutes of our first proper meetings on Teams. We really gelled well as a group and could easily allow for tangential discussions (I believe we’re all mechanical keyboard aficionados) that would probably go on for hours.
In the beginning, we were slow to find our groove as a working unit which was a place of improvement. Perhaps that was due to semester break and so this wasn’t truly a priority at the time. That was short lived and will not be repeated in A3 now that we each understand where we are aiming regarding marks. Also, we were very polite to each other at the beginning which caused stagnation. We quickly got to a point of making quicker decisions and executing.
I was surprised at how quickly we could just rely on each other to get delegated portions of work completed. I was happy when group members would reach out to the wider group for help or prod each of us along. This showed that individuals within our group had the capacity to be critical of the project and contributions and could get us working. Admittedly a place I could personally improve on is letting go of aspects of a project. When you look at the Github trail of commits, it will be heavy with my username due to my annoying tendency of making small, incremental changes, testing, checking, scrapping, and re-committing (repeat cycle forever). My colleagues made more meaningful commits and so showed less overall due to this.
Clearly, we worked well as a team in which I know our A2 submission will solidify. At the best of times a dynamic like this is difficult to find so I’m extremely pleased with our group and dynamic.
